A little bit about what we do

Developing search engines
that help organisations discover
the hidden value of vast data stores
and uncover their true potential

Mindmaps focuses on developing knowledge representation systems that allow us to integrate large datasets through knowledge ontologies. We develop our systems using graph technologies that enable us to perform parallelised semantic search on big data. The end result is a search engine that can infer meaningful relationships between previously distinct and isolated pieces of information. By leveraging these relationships, our search engine can then perform an explicit search over large amounts of data. We can assist organisations in a variety of fields and industries to find information and explore ideas in an integrative manner. Our search engine integrates five distinct fields in computer science: ontology engineering, machine learning, natural language processing, semantic search and distributed computing. Our system architecture is designed with a high degree of modularity and scalability such that it can be easily customised to function with different types of data and industries.

Our first application will be a semantic search engine aimed at the film industry that is capable of traversing the corpus of movie information on the internet to allow users to perform more nuanced search and provide more meaningful search results.
